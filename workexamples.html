Soft Skills

  • Work Ethic
  • Determination
  • Adaptability
  • Leadership
  • Teamwork
  • Time management
  • Dependability
  • Organization

I have held many jobs over my lifetime. I began work at an early age of 13 and, from that moment on, I have held a desire to make sure I give everything I have to an employer. I pride myself on my work ethic and determination. I believe having to work at such an early age while still attending school has allowed me the opportunity to balance multiple tasks. This work ethic continued through a long (12 year) military career through the Army and Marines. My work ethic and determination allowed me to progress through the ranks faster than my peers. This granted me many opportunities to gain and demonstrate leadership. In the military, you must prove your value and ability by completing courses to ensure you can be a good leader. The lives of others can be in your hands and it must be proved that you are up for this challenge. While progressing through this career, I was given a difficult task; although, it was of my own decision. I was forced to balance work with family. With a non-traditional job, this task becomes even harder. When you do not work the typical 9-5 job, you have to figure out different ways to balance your time. Spending up to 15 months at a time away from your children and spouse most times thousands of miles and many countries away, is extremely difficult. As my children have grown older and are growing into responsible people, I feel more and more confident that I have done a good job.
